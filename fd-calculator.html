What is a Fixed Deposit (FD)?

A Fixed Deposit (FD) is one of the safest and most popular investment options in India. Offered by banks, post offices, and NBFCs, an FD allows you to deposit a lump sum amount for a fixed period at a predetermined interest rate. Unlike a savings account, the interest rate on an FD is locked in for the entire tenure, giving you guaranteed returns regardless of market conditions.

Fixed Deposits are ideal for conservative investors who want capital protection and steady returns. They are backed by deposit insurance of up to ₹5 lakh per depositor per bank under DICGC, making them one of the safest investment instruments available in India.

FD Interest Calculation Formula

Fixed Deposit returns are calculated using the compound interest formula:

A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t)

Where A = Maturity amount, P = Principal amount, r = Annual interest rate (in decimal), n = Number of times interest is compounded per year, and t = Tenure in years. The interest earned is A − P.

Types of Fixed Deposits in India

1. Cumulative FD

In a cumulative FD, the interest is compounded and reinvested. You receive the principal along with all accumulated interest at maturity. This is ideal for long-term goals where you do not need regular income.

2. Non-Cumulative FD

In a non-cumulative FD, interest is paid out at regular intervals — monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or annually. This is suitable for retirees or those who need regular income from their investments.

3. Tax-Saving FD

A tax-saving FD has a lock-in period of 5 years and qualifies for deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, up to ₹1.5 lakh per year. However, the interest earned is fully taxable.

4. Senior Citizen FD

Most banks offer an additional 0.25% to 0.50% interest rate to senior citizens (aged 60 and above) on Fixed Deposits. Senior citizens also have a higher TDS exemption threshold of ₹50,000 per year on FD interest.

Current FD Interest Rates in India (2026)

FD interest rates vary across banks. As a general reference, major Indian banks currently offer rates between 6.5% to 8.5% per annum for regular customers, with slightly higher rates for senior citizens. NBFCs and small finance banks may offer higher rates ranging from 8% to 9.5%, though they carry slightly higher risk.

FD vs Other Investment Options

FD vs Savings Account

FDs offer significantly higher interest rates (6.5%–8.5%) compared to regular savings accounts (2.5%–4%). If you have idle funds you don't need immediately, an FD is a much better option than letting it sit in a savings account.

FD vs SIP (Mutual Fund)

FDs offer guaranteed returns with zero market risk, but returns are generally lower and fully taxable. SIP investments in mutual funds carry market risk but can potentially generate higher returns (10%–14%) over the long term. FDs are suitable for short-term goals, while SIPs are better for long-term wealth creation.

FD vs PPF

PPF offers tax-free returns and falls under the EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) tax category, making it more tax-efficient than FDs. However, PPF has a 15-year lock-in period, whereas FDs offer more flexibility with tenures from 7 days to 10 years.

Tips to Maximise Your FD Returns

  • Choose quarterly compounding over annual compounding for higher effective yield
  • Ladder your FDs across different tenures to balance liquidity and returns
  • Senior citizens should always book FDs under their name for the extra interest rate benefit
  • Compare rates across banks and small finance banks before booking
  • Submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS deduction if your income is below the taxable limit

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is a Fixed Deposit (FD)?

A Fixed Deposit is a financial instrument where you deposit a lump sum with a bank or NBFC for a fixed period at a predetermined interest rate, earning guaranteed returns.

2. How is FD interest calculated?

FD interest is calculated using compound interest formula: A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t), where P is principal, r is annual rate, n is compounding frequency, and t is tenure in years.

3. What is the minimum FD amount in India?

Most banks allow Fixed Deposits starting from ₹1,000. Some banks and NBFCs may have different minimum deposit requirements.

4. Is FD interest taxable in India?

Yes. FD interest is fully taxable as per your income tax slab. Banks deduct TDS at 10% if annual interest exceeds ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens).

5. What is the difference between cumulative and non-cumulative FD?

In cumulative FD, interest is compounded and paid at maturity. In non-cumulative FD, interest is paid at regular intervals — monthly, quarterly, or annually.

6. Can I withdraw my FD before maturity?

Yes, most banks allow premature withdrawal with a small penalty — usually a 0.5% to 1% reduction in the applicable interest rate.
